Premchand is the most famous Hindi novelist and Godaan is
Premchand’s most celebrated novel. Economic and social
in a north Indian village are brilliantly captured in the
story of Hori, a poor farmer and his family’s struggle for
survival and self-respect. Hori does everything he can to fulfill
his life’s desire: to own a cow, the peasant’s measure of wealth
and well-being. Like many Hindus of his time, he believes that
making the gift of a cow to a Brahman before he dies will help
him achieve salvation. An engaging introduction to India before
Independence, Godaan is at once village ethnography, moving
human document and insightful colonial history.
